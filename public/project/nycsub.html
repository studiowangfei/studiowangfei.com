New York City Sub Rider-Readers

Most of time data visualization targets on the crowd as a whole, which leaves personal touch behind. NYC Sub Rider-Readers tries to bridge the data of "whole" and the data of "one". The project is comprised of two parts: one is the visualization of weekday, weekend and annual riderships of every New York subway stations, which represents the movement of subway riders across the city; the other is tracking subway readers - who constitute a fairly amount of daily commuters - and generating personal travelling maps of their reading behaviors. Using digital data collected by ticket checking machine and physical mapping rendered by photographs and drawings, the project delivers an image of contemporary moving and reading in metropolis.

Data source: Metropolitan Transportation Authority of New York, Underground New York Public Library
