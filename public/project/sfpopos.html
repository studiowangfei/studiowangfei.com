I started this project 3 months after I moved to San Francisco, mainly because of my strong interest in cities, data and people's behaviors.

It's still under development... Stay tuned!

Context
POPOS stands for Privately Owned Public Open Space. POPOS are publicly accessible spaces in forms of plazas, terraces, atriums, small parks, and even snippets that are provided and maintained by private property owners. In San Francisco, POPOS mostly appear in the Downtown office district area. Private developers provide POPOS under three general circumstances: voluntarily, in exchange for a density bonus, or as a condition of approval.

Design Challenge
The information and geo-data of San Francisco's POPOS are not well gathered and structured, which means the first step will be creating the database and mapping out all POPOS. Another challenge will be creating clear and easy-to-understand visuals of space's shadow and sunlight conditions, which asks to go beyond 2D representation and bring in 3D visualization.
2D to 3D
Using the City Modeling data of San Francisco and the annual solar data from ESRL, a 2D POPO's shadow map was created as the first step, then moved to a 3D building shadow map.
POPOS Finder & Sunshine Teller
An app is envisioned and designed for general public users to view overall SF POPOS info, locate a specific POPOS, and learn about real-time sunlight/shadow information about it. The app is cross-platform based (web prototype is here, mobile coming soon...) and currently under development.
