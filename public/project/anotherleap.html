Another Leap
Another Leap.
Many minds. Many hands.
As a collective response to the digital shift of our society, the exhibition invites artists, makers, engineers, designers, and entrepreneurs to deliver projects inspired by emerging social and technological phenomena such as big data, maker culture, freelancing, e-commerce, we-media, robotics, labor migration, etc.
Tiny. Massive. Bottom-up.
As an exhibition unit of the 10th Shanghai Biennale, Another Leap was designed and curated as a cultural event to trigger the tiny, massive and bottom-up power from online and offline crowd. It captured nearly one million visitors in four months and was reported by over twenty media agencies. The manifesto of Another Leap was printed over fouty thousand copies and out of stock in first two weeks of the show.

Manifesto

This leap is Another Leap.

Compared to "Great Leap Forward" implemented by the Communist Party of China in 1958, and "one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind" by Neil Armstrong in 1969, Another Leap is a tiny, massive, and bottom-up one. It is a series of collective actions based on individual will. It is a direct practice of "Two heads are better than one", an empirical iteration of "1+1>2". It is a collective leap pushed forward by many minds and many hands.
